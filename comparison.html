Comparisons Across Weather Elements

Cloudiness by Latitude


There is no relationship between cloudiness and distance from the equator. The correlation coefficient for the Northern Hemisphere Latitude by Cloudiness at 0.1 and Southern Hemisphere by Cloudiness is 0.0.

Humidity by Latitude


There is a weak relationship between humidity and latitude in the Northern Hemisphere (correlation coefficient is .32), and a very weak/no relationship between humidity and latitude in the Southern Hemisphere (correlation coefficiet is .05).

Temperature by Latitude


Temperatures increase in cities nearer to the equator and decrease in cities farther from the equator, as indicated by the shape of the City Latitude by Temperature Scatter plot.

There is a stronger negative relationship between temperatures and latitude in the Northern hemisphere than the positive relationship between temperatures and latitude in the Southern hemisphere, indicated by the correlation coefficient in the Northern Hemisphere Latitude by Temperature at -.88 and the correlation coefficient in the Southern Hemisphere Latitude by Temperature at .62.

Wind by Latitude


There is a weak relationship between wind speed and distance from the equator, as indicated by the correlation coefficient in the Northern Hemisphere Latitute by Wind Speed at 0.17 and the correlation coefficient in the Southern Hemisphere Latitude by Wind Speed at -.26. These correlation coefficients suggest that there is a weak trend that wind speeds increase as the distance from the equater increases.
